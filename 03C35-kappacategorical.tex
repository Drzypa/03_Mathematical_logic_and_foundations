Let $L$ be a first order language and let $S$ be a set of $L$-sentences.  If $\kappa$ is a cardinal, then $S$ is said to be \emph{\PMlinkescapetext{$\kappa$-categorical}} if $S$ has a model of cardinality $\kappa$ and any two such models are isomorphic.

In other words, $S$ is categorical iff it has a unique model of cardinality $\kappa$, to within isomorphism.
