\paragraph{Definition}
A \emph{functional} $T$ is a function mapping a function space (often a vector space) $V$ in to a field of scalars $K$, typically taken to be $\R$ or $\mathbb{C}$.  

\paragraph{Discussion}
Examples of functionals include the integral and entropy.  A functional $f$ is often indicated by the use of \PMlinkescapetext{square} brackets, $T[x]$ rather than $T(x)$.

The \emph{linear functionals} are those functionals $T$ that \PMlinkescapetext{satisfy}
\begin{itemize}
\item $T(x+y)=T(x)+T(y)$
\item $T(cx)=cT(x)$
\end{itemize}
for any $c\in K$, $x,y\in V$.
