inverse image


Let f:A⟶B be a function, and let U⊂B be a subset. The inverse image of U is the set f-1⁢(U)⊂A consisting of all elements a∈A such that f⁢(a)∈U.

The inverse image commutes with all set operations: For any collection {Ui}i∈I of subsets of B, we have the following identities for

  1. 1.

    Unions:

    f-1⁢(⋃i∈IUi)=⋃i∈If-1⁢(Ui)
  2. 2.

    Intersections:

    f-1⁢(⋂i∈IUi)=⋂i∈If-1⁢(Ui)

and for any subsets U and V of B, we have identities for

  1. 3.

    Complements:

    (f-1⁢(U))∁=f-1⁢(U∁)
  2. 4.

    Set differences:

    f-1⁢(U∖V)=f-1⁢(U)∖f-1⁢(V)
  3. 5.

    Symmetric differences:

    f-1⁢(U△V)=f-1⁢(U)△f-1⁢(V)

In addition, for X⊂A and Y⊂B, the inverse image satisfies the miscellaneous identities

  1. 6.

    (f|X)-1⁢(Y)=X∩f-1⁢(Y)

  2. 7.

    f⁢(f-1⁢(Y))=Y∩f⁢(A)

  3. 8.

    X⊂f-1⁢(f⁢(X)), with equality if f is injective.
