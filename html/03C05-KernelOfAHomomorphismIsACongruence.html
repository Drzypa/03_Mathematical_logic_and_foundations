kernel of a homomorphism is a congruence


Let Σ be a fixed signature, and 𝔄 and 𝔅 two structures for Σ. If f:𝔄→𝔅 is a homomorphism, then ker⁡(f) is a congruence on 𝔄.

Proof.

If F is an n-ary function symbol of Σ, and f⁢(ai)=f⁢(ai′), then

f⁢(F𝔄⁢(a1,…,an)) =F𝔅⁢(f⁢(a1),…,f⁢(an))
=F𝔅⁢(f⁢(a1′),…,f⁢(an′))
=f⁢(F𝔄⁢(a1′,…,an′)).∎
