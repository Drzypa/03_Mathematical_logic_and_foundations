well-ordering principle for natural numbers proven from the principle of finite induction


Let S be a nonempty set of natural numbers. We show that there is an a∈S such that for all b∈S, a≤b. Suppose not, then

(*)    ∀a∈S,∃b∈S b<a.

We will use the principle of finite induction (the strong form) to show that S is empty, a contradition.

Fix any natural number n, and suppose that for all natural numbers m<n, m∈ℕ∖S. If n∈S, then (*) implies that there is an element b∈S such that b<n. This would be incompatible with the assumption that for all natural numbers m<n, m∈ℕ∖S. Hence, we conclude that n is not in S.

Therefore, by induction, no natural number is a member of S. The set is empty.
