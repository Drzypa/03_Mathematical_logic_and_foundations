cycle


Let S be a set. A cycle is a permutation (bijective function of a set onto itself) such that there exist distinct elements a1,a2,…,ak of S such that

f⁢(ai)=ai+1  and  f⁢(ak)=a1

that is

f⁢(a1) = a2
f⁢(a2) = a3
⋮
f⁢(ak) = a1

and f⁢(x)=x for any other element of S.

This can also be pictured as

a1↦a2↦a3↦⋯↦ak↦a1

and

x↦x

for any other element x∈S, where ↦ represents the action of f.

One of the basic results on symmetric groups says that any finite permutation can be expressed as product of disjoint cycles.
