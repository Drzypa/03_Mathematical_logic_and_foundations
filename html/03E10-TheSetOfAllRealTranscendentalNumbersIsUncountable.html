Theorem.The set of all real transcendental numbers is uncountable.

Proof. Denote 𝕋 and 𝔸 be the set of real transcendental and real algebraic numbers respectively. Suppose 𝕋 is countable. Then the union 𝕋∪𝔸=ℝ is also countable, since 𝔸 is also countable, which is a contradiction. Therefore 𝕋 must be uncountable. □
