fibre


Given a function f:X⟶Y, a fibre is an inverse image of an element of Y. That is given y∈Y, f-1⁢({y})={x∈X∣f⁢(x)=y} is a fibre.

Example: Define f:ℝ2⟶ℝ by f⁢(x,y)=x2+y2. Then the fibres of f consist of concentric circles about the origin, the origin itself, and empty sets depending on whether we look at the inverse image of a positive number, zero, or a negative number respectively.

Example: Suppose M is a manifold, and π:T⁢M→M is the canonical projection from the tangent bundle T⁢M to M. Then fibres of π are the tangent spaces Tx⁢(M) for x∈M.
