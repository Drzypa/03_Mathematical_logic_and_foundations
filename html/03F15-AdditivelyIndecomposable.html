additively indecomposable


An ordinal α is called additively indecomposable if it is not 0 and for any β,γ<α, we have β+γ<α. The set of additively indecomposable ordinals is denoted ℍ.

Obviously 1∈ℍ, since 0+0<1. No finite ordinal other than 1 is in ℍ. Also, ω∈ℍ, since the sum of two finite ordinals is still finite. More generally, every infinite cardinal is in ℍ.

ℍ is closed and unbounded, so the enumerating function of ℍ is normal. In fact, fℍ⁢(α)=ωα.
