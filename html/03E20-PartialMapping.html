partial mapping


Let X1,⋯,Xn and Y be sets, and let f be a function of n variables: f:X1×X2×⋯×Xn→Y. xi∈Xi for 2≤i≤n. The induced mapping a↦f⁢(a,x2,…,xn) is called the partial mapping determined by f corresponding to the first variable.

In the case where n=2, the map defined by a↦f⁢(a,x) is often denoted f⁢(⋅,x). Further, any function f:X1×X2→Y determines a mapping from X1 into the set of mappings of X2 into Y, namely f¯:x↦(y↦f(x,y)). The converse holds too, and it is customary to identify f with f¯. Many of the “canonical isomorphisms” that we come across (e.g. in multilinear algebra) are illustrations of this kind of identification.
