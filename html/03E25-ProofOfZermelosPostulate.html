proof of Zermelo’s postulate


The following is a proof that the axiom of choice implies Zermelo’s postulate.

Proof.

Let ℱ be a disjoint family of nonempty sets. Let f:ℱ→⋃ℱ be a choice function. Let A,B∈ℱ with A≠B. Since ℱ is a disjoint family of sets, A∩B=∅. Since f is a choice function, f⁢(A)∈A and f⁢(B)∈B. Thus, f⁢(A)∉B. Hence, f⁢(A)≠f⁢(B). It follows that f is injective.

Let C={f⁢(B)∈⋃ℱ:B∈ℱ}. Then C is a set.

Let A∈ℱ. Since f is injective, A∩C={f⁢(A)}. ∎
