10.1.1 Limits and colimits


Since sets are closed under products, the universal property of products in \autorefthm:prod-ump shows immediately that 𝒮⁢e⁢t has finite products. In fact, infinite products follow just as easily from the equivalence

(X→∏a:AB(a))≃(∏a:A(X→B(a))).

And we saw in \autorefex:pullback that the pullback of f:A→C and g:B→C can be defined as ∑(a:A)∑(b:B)f⁢(a)=g⁢(b); this is a set if A,B,C are and inherits the correct universal property. Thus, 𝒮⁢e⁢t is a complete category in the obvious sense.

Since sets are closed under + and contain 𝟎, 𝒮⁢e⁢t has finite coproducts. Similarly, since ∑(a:A)B⁢(a) is a set whenever A and each B⁢(a) are, it yields a coproduct of the family B in 𝒮⁢e⁢t. Finally, we showed in \autorefsec:pushouts that pushouts exist in n-types, which includes 𝒮⁢e⁢t in particular. Thus, 𝒮⁢e⁢t is also cocomplete.
